ABOUT

Everyone knows this man, maybe not for his name or his face but his magnificent compositions. Ennio Morricone has been one of the best musician of our decade and personally my favorite film composer. Here's my tribute web page for him..He has written music for more than 500 motion pictures and TV series and Morricone's music has been used in more than 60 award-winning films.

Morricone has received five Oscar nominations, the first in 1979 for the soundtrack of the film "Days of Heaven" by Terrence Malick, then follow that for "Mission" by Roland Joffé, 1986, "The Untouchables "Brian De Palma in 1987," Bugsy "Barry Lavison in 1992 and" Malena "by Giuseppe Tornatore in 2001.
Morricone has lived in Italy his entire life and has never desired to live in Hollywood. Morricone is also not fluent in English and will give interviews only in Italian, his native language. To know more about his music, concerts, tour dates and biography, please, check his website here

TIMELINE

  • Born in Rome on November 10, 1928
  • Morricone attendend at the age of twelve at the Conservatory of Santa Cecilia.
  • The first soundtrack was for the movie “A fistful of dollars”.
  • His first teacher was his father Mario Morricone, who taught him how to read music and also to play several instruments.
  • entered the National Academy of St Cecilia, to take trumpet lessons under the guidance of Umberto Semproni
  • Morricone formally entered the conservatory in 1940 at age 12
  • He studied the trumpet, composition, and choral music, under direction of Goffredo Petrassi
  • In 1946, he composed "Il Mattino" ("The Morning") for voice and piano on a text by Fukuko, first in a group of seven "youth" Lieder.
  • In 1953, Morricone was asked by Gorni Kramer and Lelio Luttazzi to write an arrangement for some medleys in an American style for a series of evening radio shows.
  • In 1962, Morricone worked with American jazz singer Helen Merrill as an arranger on an EP "Helen Merrill sings Italian Songs" on the RCA Italiana label
  • In 1987, Morricone co-wrote 'It Couldn't Happen Here' with the Pet Shop Boys.
  • In 2006, "The Ecstasy of Gold" became one of Morricone's best-known compositions. The opening scene of Jeff Tremaine's Jackass Number Two.
